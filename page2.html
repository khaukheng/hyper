Treatment Options

Option #1: Anti-thyroid Drugs

Treatment: Thionamides medicines
To treat an overactive thyroid by stopping thyroid to produce excess hormones.
Main types: Methimazole, Carbimazole and Propylthiouracil (PTU)
Usual first choice: Methimazole, as PTU carries higher risk(still low overall) of liver damage
Dosage & duration:
Take daily for a month or two before notice any improvement in condition.

Option #2: Radioactive Iodine (RAI)

Treatment: Radiation
To damage thyroid, in order to limit the amounnt of hormones it can produce. Given a drink or apsule that contains a low dose of radiation, which is then absorbed by your thyroid. Mostly it's single treatment.
Duration: Few weeks or months
Before the full benefits to be felt, may need to take one of these medications (methimazole, arbimazole and propylthiouracil (PTU)) for a short time.
Cautions:
Not recommended if:
  • Pregnant
  • Breastfeeding
  • Have confirmed or suspected thyroid cancer
  • Plannig a pregnancy within 4-6 months

Option #3: Thyroid Surgery (thyroidectomy)

Treatment: Surgery
Remove part or all of the thyroid gland
Recommendation: Remove entire thyroid gland
No chance of the symptoms coming back
